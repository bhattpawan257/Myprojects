\sectiontitle{Insights \& Observations}{neoncyan}

\begin{tcolorbox}[enhanced, colback=cardbg, colframe=cardbg, arc=4mm, boxrule=0pt, drop shadow]
    \large
    \begin{itemize}[itemsep=0.6cm, label=\textcolor{neoncyan}{$\bullet$}]
        \item \textbf{Mathematics proved highly challenging:} The average score in Mathematics hovered around 52\%, with a large number of PCM students scoring in the 40s and 50s. This heavily impacted the aggregate percentage of the PCM cohort.
        \item \textbf{PCB (Biology) outperformed PCM:} Biology students maintained a higher aggregate average (approx. 79.2\%), driven by strong performances in Biology (Avg: 88\%) and Hindi Core.
        \item \textbf{Language subjects acted as grade saviors:} Hindi Core (Avg: 85\%) and English Core (Avg: 75\%) witnessed extremely consistent, high scores across all streams, preventing overall percentage drops.
        \item \textbf{Computer Science is a major strength:} Students opting for Computer Science scored remarkably well, averaging near 89\%, making it the highest-scoring elective in the entire batch.
        \item \textbf{Commerce stream polarity:} While Economics yielded excellent scores (Avg: 75\%), Accountancy proved difficult for the Commerce batch, with many students scoring below 60, dragging the stream average to 73.5\%.
        \item \textbf{Topper Demographics:} The top ranks are heavily mixed, with the overall School Topper emerging from the Commerce stream (Manish Kumar - 89.0\%), closely followed by PCM (Rudransh Singh - 86.6\%) and PCB (Nikita Joshi - 85.2\%).
    \end{itemize}
